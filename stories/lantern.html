The Lantern of Luminous Dreams

In a small village nestled at the foot of the Misty Mountains, there was a curious tradition: each child, upon turning sixteen, was given a lantern. But these weren't ordinary lanterns. They were said to hold the power to reveal a person’s deepest, unspoken dream whenever lit under the new moon.

Mira had grown up hearing the stories, but like most teenagers, she thought it was all just a fairy tale. Until, on the night of her sixteenth birthday, she was handed her own lantern by the village elder. It was ancient, its brass exterior intricately carved with symbols she couldn't understand. As she held it, a strange warmth spread through her fingers, and for a moment, she felt as though the lantern pulsed in her hands, alive.

The elder’s words echoed in her mind: "Light it only when the moon is hidden, and it will reveal what your heart truly seeks."

Curious but skeptical, Mira placed the lantern by her bedside, resolving to ignore the odd tradition. But that night, as the new moon rose high and the village slumbered, Mira woke with an overwhelming urge to light the lantern. The room was so dark it felt like the shadows were pressing in from all sides, but she could see the faint outline of the lantern, almost glowing on its own.

She lit the wick.

As soon as the flame flickered to life, it wasn’t just light that filled the room—but memories, dreams, and forgotten whispers. The walls around her dissolved, and she found herself standing in a place she'd never been before: a magnificent city made of glass and gold, floating above a sea of stars.

In this city, people moved like shadows, weightless and free. And among them, Mira saw someone familiar—a young boy, with a laugh she recognized deep in her soul. It was her brother, Soren. Except Soren had disappeared five years ago. Everyone had assumed he was lost to the wilds beyond the village, but here he was, alive, in this ethereal place.

Mira tried to call out, but her voice was swallowed by the dream-world’s silence. She ran toward him, her heart pounding, but the closer she got, the more the city began to fade. The stars around her flickered, and just as her fingers grazed the edge of Soren’s sleeve, the vision vanished.

Mira found herself back in her room, her breath shallow, her heart racing, the lantern burning low. She knew now: Soren wasn’t gone. He was trapped in that city of dreams, somewhere beyond the world she knew.

And she had to find him.
